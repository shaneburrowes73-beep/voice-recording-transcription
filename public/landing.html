The Problem

Generic transcription fails Caribbean professionals

Off-the-shelf tools were trained on American and British English — not how we speak in Barbados.

🎙️

High error rates on Caribbean accents

Standard STT engines misread Barbadian intonation, dialect vocabulary and Creole expressions, producing transcripts that need heavy manual correction.

⛓️

No chain of custody

Law enforcement and legal teams need every transcript to be reviewed, approved and locked by a supervisor. Consumer tools offer none of this.

🔓

Sensitive data in shared platforms

Police incident notes and legal records cannot share infrastructure with other organisations. Off-the-shelf tools cannot guarantee this isolation.

📋

Manual transcription wastes time

Officers spending 20–30 minutes typing up notes they already spoke out loud is time taken away from the work that matters.

How It Works

Record. Correct. Approve. Done.

Four steps from spoken word to locked, approved record.

1

Tap Record

Officer taps the microphone button and speaks naturally. Live transcript appears in real time.

2

AI corrects

Grammar and spelling are automatically cleaned up. Barbadian dialect expressions are preserved correctly.

3

Review & submit

Officer reviews the corrected text, adds a case reference, and submits for supervisor approval.

4

Supervisor approves

Supervisor reviews, adds notes if needed, and approves or rejects. Every action is logged with timestamp and identity.
